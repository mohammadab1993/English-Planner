The daily routine

Fixed, every day, in this order. ~2 hours of active work.

10 MIN

Grammar

One micro-topic from Headway. Write 3 example sentences of your own.

10 MIN

Shadowing

Repeat 1 minute of native audio out loud, 8–10 times. Copy rhythm, not just words.

20 MIN

Vocabulary

WordSkills. New words only inside full sentences. Review yesterday's first.

25 MIN

Listening

BBC 6 Minute / Tactics for Listening. Listen twice: once blind, once with transcript.

20 MIN

Reading

Graded reader, article, or review. Underline 5 useful phrases, not single words.

20 MIN

Output

Mirror speaking or writing — alternate days. Always about today's input.

ALL DAY · PASSIVE

Background input

English music while commuting, one series episode in the evening, phone in English. Passive input doesn't count as study — it counts as environment.

The 12-week plan

Three phases. Tap a week to open its missions and check them off.

Foundation · A1 → A2. Goal: train your ear and mouth on learner-friendly material. Series: Extra English (made for learners). Book: Headway Elementary. Everything simple, everything daily.
Expansion · A2 → B1. Goal: move to real native content. Series: Friends S1 with English subtitles. Book: Headway Pre-Intermediate + your first graded reader (romance is fine — pleasure keeps you reading).
Interview Mode · B1 → B2. Goal: fluency under pressure. Friends without subtitles, real articles in your field, and structured interview answers using the STAR method (Situation → Task → Action → Result).

Three rules of the system

The plan works only if these are non-negotiable.

RULE 1

Record every Sunday

2 minutes of free speaking, saved with the date. Comparing week 1 to week 6 is the only honest progress meter.

RULE 2

No zero days

A bad day means 20 minutes of shadowing, not nothing. The streak matters more than any single session.

RULE 3

Phrases, not words

Never memorize a word alone. Memorize the sentence it lives in. Your brain stores chunks, not dictionary entries.
